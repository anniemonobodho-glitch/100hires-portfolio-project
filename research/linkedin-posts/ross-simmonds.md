**Topic:** Off-Site B2B Buyer Journeys and Third-Party AI Grounding.

**Brief summary:**  
Ross Simmonds highlights that the modern B2B buying decision happens almost entirely off-site before a prospect ever hits your domain. Buyers—and the AI models summarizing answers for them—rely heavily on Reddit threads, G2 reviews, and YouTube teardowns to form their shortlists. To stay visible, brands must actively participate in these third-party spaces by standing up transparent Reddit presences, optimizing their existing YouTube catalogs, and using customer reviews for actual product R&D rather than just reputation management.

**Why this is useful for the playbook:**  
Shifts Focus to Off-Site Grounding: Proves to CMOs that optimizing your own website is no longer enough; the playbook must include strategies for the "rooms you do not own" because that is the exact training data LLMs use to build their answers.

Provides Actionable Community Tactics: Gives your team concrete mandates—like updating historical YouTube videos and engaging authentically in niche subreddits—to ensure the brand naturally appears when buyers ask AI for vendor recommendations.

Redefines Reputation Management: Teaches teams to treat third-party discussions not just as a PR problem, but as high-intent Bottom of Funnel (BOFU) content opportunities that give AI models a clean, credible reason to cite the brand.

**Topic:** B2B SaaS YouTube SERP Domination and Bottom-of-Funnel Video SEO.

**Brief summary:**  
Ross Simmonds shares proprietary research analyzing over 8,000 B2B SaaS keywords, revealing that YouTube dominates traditional Google search results for commercial and transactional intents. Specifically, YouTube videos show up 80% of the time for product demos and 20–22% of the time for category reviews, comparison queries (Brand vs Brand), and "best of" lists. To exploit this, brands must treat YouTube as an intent-matching keyword engine by optimizing historical content footprints and systematically deploying value-driven video series targeting high-priority keywords.

**Why this is useful for the playbook:**  
Redirects Budget to High-Intent Real Estate: Instead of exclusively competing for ultra-expensive, double-digit CPC Google Ad keywords, it shifts your playbook's focus toward gaining zero-cost organic real estate on YouTube where buyers are already looking for product validation.

Provides a Historical Video Optimization Framework: Establishes an immediate, high-ROI workflow for your design and content teams: updating old video titles, applying high-contrast "creator-style" thumbnails, and rewriting short descriptions to instantly recover latent organic impressions.

Feeds the Multi-Channel AI Grounding Engine: Because Google owns YouTube and feeds its video data directly into AI Overviews and search-grounding architectures, building highly optimized, intent-focused video assets inherently scales your brand's authority across modern AI answer engines.
